The Hennessy cognac distillery was founded by Irish Jacobite military officer Richard Hennessy in 1765, who had served in the army of Louis XV. He retired to the Cognac region, and began distilling and exporting brandies, first to Britain and his native Ireland, closely followed by the United States.

In celebration of the 50th anniversary of Hip Hop and the 50th birthday of long time partner Nas, Hennessy commissioned renowned artist, Kennedy Yanko to create a one of one tribute piece to gift the icon. The sculpture features a custom Hennessy V.S bottle design to commemorate the milestone occasion. Kennedy Yanko shares, “The expression found in the lyrics and medium of Hip Hop are soft and hard, have gravity and transmute experiences. In this way, my tribute piece—created from soft and hard, heavy and enduring materials—embodies and honors the movement. It embodies what I think of when I reflect on Nas, his constitution, his contributions and his legacy.

While paradise is a place of creation where nature becomes sublime, in Cognac, France, the Hennessy cellars are another legendary place where the rarest eaux-de-vie patently mature to become divine. Out of one of the world's largest collections of eaux-de-vie at Hennessy, only a very small amount of eaux-de-vie mature in oak barrels in these cellars. The stock dates back to 1774. Selected for their character and immense potential, these eaux-de-vie have been carefully looked after by eight generations of Master Blenders. These barrels lose a small amount of liquid each year due to natural evaporation, called the angels' share. Not to fear, as this divine intervention transforms the character of eaux-de-vie. Inspired by symphonic music and emotional connection, Hennessy Paradis is a universally loved blend: it is voluptuous and harmonious and combines smoothness and strength with a silky and graceful texture. Influenced by the world's natural beauty and the cognac's own roots in symphonic harmony, every "Paradis is on Earth" moment is a transporting experience suspended by music
